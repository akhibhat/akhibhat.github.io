Madurai mein jigar thanda!

The view we woke up to!

The huffing and puffing of the Mysore-Tuticorin express woke us up to a picturesque view of the Western Ghats. We were approaching our next pit stop - Madurai. For the only two days during our 42 day expedition, we were going to be totally relaxed. We were to be hosted by one of our friend's parents who were going to show us around. Uncle had come to pick us up in their car. By this time we were so used to getting down the train, picking up our bags and walking all the way to the hotel that going in a car felt like a 'luxury'. When we reached home, Aunty welcomed us with delicious, soft idlis along with the spicy coriander chutney and hot tea. She had also planned the entire two days for us.

After having the delicious breakfast, we left with uncle for a small hike to the Azhagar Malai (Azhagar hills). It is situated around 20km north of the city of Madurai. At the foot of the mountains is a temple of Lord Vishnu.

Lord Vishnu temple at the foothills of Azhagar Malai

A short trek through the dense vegetation took us to the top of the mountain, on which is situated the Pazhamudircholai - a temple dedicated to Lord Murugan (Lord Kartikeya).

Uncle, who was a Tamil patriot and had immense knowledge about Tamil history and literature, told us a legend about the temple as to how the great Tamil poet Avvaiyar had been tested and humbled by Lord Murugan.

The legend... in Tamil though

A peaceful prayer and an offering of prasadam each and we were on our way back to the foothills to get back home for lunch.

That day we had the best lunch of our trip. Aunty had prepared chicken for us along with dal rice. By the time we were done, none of us could move.

Lunch ‚ù§

After allowing our tummies some time to digest, we set out again for the Meenakshi Amman temple. The scale of construction and architecture left us spellbound. The temple had a pond situated in the centre and 14 gopurams in different locations. This pond had a golden lotus floating in it.

The temple - the gopurams and the pond

By the time we had finished capturing different places of the temple on our cameras, it was time to pick Aunty from her office. On our way, we stopped to taste the famous drink of Madurai - the Jigarthanda. It translates to 'cool heart' in English and boy it proved true to its name. The drink cooled off all the exhaustion from roaming around in the hot sun.

Jigarthanda!!!

After picking Aunty, we decided to taste Idiyappam, another South Indian delicacy, for dinner. We had a nice dinner at home and soon went to sleep as a long journey awaited us early morning.

Idiyappam - also called Semige in Kannada

The next day, we left early morning at 5:30 am for a trip to see the Courtallam Falls. It was a four hour drive from home. On our way, we got a taste of the creativity of South Indian people. We stopped to taste another of South India's famous drinks called Padneer. It is the extract of the palm trees. The Padneer was served in a cup made out of palm leaves itself. We were amazed by the creativity of the people. They had used all the parts of the palm trees so effectively! Along with its fruit, it was so good and filling, that we did not need breakfast!

The container made from palm tree leaves

The falls were majestic, the water was cold and pure. It tasted of the grass and the rocks that it passed through before reaching down. We were amazed to see the number of people that had gathered to bathe in the falls. After a short visit to a temple later, we left for the journey back home as we were to leave that night.
