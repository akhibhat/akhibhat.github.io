The Sea, Sand, Beaches and much more...

Rucksacks packed, itineraries prepared, three young excited minds unaware of the many experiences that were to come, we were to start our journey from Mumbai â€“ the city of dreams! What a coincidence! The city of dreams to start our dream journey!

Coming from three different cities, the three of us met in Mumbai to board the Mumbai-Mangalore express heading towards Goa. Sitting in the sleeper bogie that night, were three young, excited souls, nervous with a mind full of so many weird plans and expectations. It felt as if none of us was going to stop talking that night. Even though we slept really late, the lashing rains, the chilly winds and the petrichor, ensured that all of us were up by 6am.

DAY 1

As we stepped out of the Madgaon railway station, we were swarmed by an eager herd of auto wallahs, who were willing to give us a ride. We had been admonished that such a situation would crop up and that it would be better to take a bus to Calangute. We tried to avoid all these wooers but they were willing to offer something which a bus ride would not. A quick, relaxed and a non-stop journey to our hotel instead of walking up to the bus stop, waiting for the bus and then a slow journey filled with regular stops. When we ignored the herd saying we would be taking the bus, one of them stepped forward and started calculating the fares for both auto and bus. Impressed by the math, we shook our heads and handed him Rs.800 for the ride. We later realized our folly and had learnt our first lesson on the first day of the trip itself.

LESSON 1: NEVER GET FOOLED BY AUTO WALLAHS. ALWAYS OPT FOR STATE TRANSPORT!

Hungry as we were, we immediately kept our luggage and went out to have lunch. We were eager to taste the Goan cuisine and hence ordered a chicken vindaloo. Here is a word of caution. If you are not fond of hot and sour food, never try Goan cuisine! DAY 2

We started off early in the morning for a trip to Old Goa. Old Goa, also known as Velha Goa (old in Portuguese), is a historical city in North Goa. It served as the capital of Portuguese India and hence boasts of a Portuguese influence on its architecture.

Learning from the previous dayâ€™s experience, we obediently took a bus to the iconic city and we were not disappointed. The journey through the bylanes, the small local houses with large courtyards, the coconut trees, and not to forget, the view of the Mandovi river on both sides as you go across the Mandovi bridge.

Vast courtyards of local houses along the route

As we walked past the Gandhi circle, it was as if we were transported to a completely different country. We saw two of the magnificent churches ever. On our right was the Basilica of Bom Jesus and on our left was the Church of St. Francis of Assisi. The grandeur of the two relics left us wondering which to explore first. The pure white marble, the exquisite green lawns and the splendid Portuguese-styled architecture, had us completely spellbound.

St. Francis of Assisi church, Velha Goa

Basilica of Bom Jesus

When you go to Goa with your friends, it is customary that you go to the Chapora Fort. Yes! The iconic location where the Bollywood classic, Dil Chahta Hai was shot.

The road to Chapora Fort

Unfortunately, the fort which was once a Portuguese stronghold against the Marathas, now lies in ruins. Now, it is just a barren space of stones and bushes.

The ruins of Chapora Fort

A long bus journey back to Calangute was followed by a stroll along the windy Calangute beach. After a calculated dinner, we decided to walk back to our hotels; not because we wanted to, but because first, it was already too late till we had our dinner, second, it had started raining heavily so finding an auto was impossible and third, we felt guilty of spending too much in the first two days itself. ðŸ˜›
