Namma Sundara Bengaluru!!

As the 56237 Mysore Bangalore Passenger train, docked in at platform 9 of the KSR Bangalore City Junction, we could see the city being lashed by the Rain God's wrath. People could be seen hurrying, with umbrellas and jackets to protect them. Some took shelter under the nearest roof while some just accepted their fate and waded through the collecting water. Cursing our timing, we started walking to our hotel, Sairam Residency, with heavy backpacks on our anterior as well as our posterior. We crossed highways and main roads, and walked through narrow by-lanes. The walk which was supposed to be just 15 minutes seemed like a lifetime. By the time we reached our hotel, we were soaked and completely exhausted. At that point, if the rooms had been like the ones in Gokarna, we would have surely broken down. Luckily, they were one of the best rooms we got on our trip. We ordered some fried rice and slept off while watching a UEFA Euro Cup match.

Our stop in Bangalore was just for a day. Kind of a rest-stop before another hectic schedule of the trip. In the morning the next day, we got up late and went for a nice breakfast. We were going to see the movie 'Udta Punjab' and then we were going to travel to different parts of Bangalore just to see the metropolitan city.

The unique Metro sign

One of the best parts about Bangalore is its 'Namma Metro' system, meaning 'Our Metro'. The metro system which is fast, comparably clean and less crowded, connects almost all the important parts of Bangalore. There are frequent trains from every station that give you a view of the city from above. We took the metro from Kempegowda Station to the Trinity station to go to Lido Mall where we were supposed to see the movie. We had already booked our tickets hence we entered the theater in no time. The movie was about the effect of drugs on the state of Punjab and its people. Although it was a dark movie, the issue tackled in it was very intriguing.

After the movie, we decided to find a particular dish which my father had recommended - the Family Dosa, which is a 4-5 ft long dosa. We roamed around trying to find it but to no avail. The search had made us very hungry so we decided to eat at a McDonald's or a KFC. On our way to the McDonald's, we found a place called 'Holige Mane' which in Kannada means 'Puranpoli house'. The aroma of the freshly made holige was very enticing and we decided to give it a shot. Although it was a very small shop,they had managed to offer so many different varieties: coconut holige, badami holige, carrot holige, mawa holige etc. And when we tasted it, it was pure bliss. The holige just melted in our mouths. We ordered a few more and by the time we were full, we had shared almost 5 holiges among us.

After some more walking around along MG Road, we were hungry again. We then went to a KFC nearby and had some delicious and crispy chicken.

Cubbon Park

After a filling meal, we decided to go to see the iconic Chinnaswamy stadium. For this, we had to go to the Cubbon Park metro station. When you get out of Cubbon park, you feel that you have entered a completely different world. It had dense trees, wide and clean roads, tall buildings and a distinct aura of poshness. A walk in this area was enough to drive away all our exhaustion. We visited a book store there and then went to the Hard Rock Cafe to watch a musical. And no, he hadn't realized it yet.
