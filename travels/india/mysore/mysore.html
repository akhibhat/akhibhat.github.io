Mysore Diaries! All about Zostel, Mylari and the Chamundi Hills :)

Its 4am in the morning at 196 Englewood Ave. Buffalo NY.

A Saturday night after a grueling week of reading numerous research papers and here I am, transported back to somewhere between Gokarna and Mysore on June 21, 2016.

In about two hours we were about to reach Mysore. A 12 hour bus journey wasn't enough to dampen our spirits, especially after having had one of the best dinners at a dhaba the previous night.

We had booked bunk beds at Zostel, Mysore and were very excited after having read some great reviews. For those of you who do not know, Zostel is a chain of backpacker's hostels started by aspiring entrepreneurs from IITs and IIMs. A 1km walk from the bus stop and we were confronted by the locked gates of the place we were supposed to stay in. Fortunately we met the caretaker of the place - a person whom we will always remember for a very good reason. He was an amiable person who showed us around the place and made us feel comfortable. He even suggested a few good places to roam around in Mysore. We certainly owe the best breakfast of our entire trip to him.

The original Mylari

It was an extremely compact place called ‚ÄúMylari original‚Äù with another restaurant of the same name right in front of it. Which of them is really the original, you'll never know as they claim to sell the best idlis and dosas. And boy they do! I have no words to describe the quality of the dosas and idlis there. The idlis were super soft that they just melted in my mouth. The dosas, crisp yet tender were fluffy and were served with a huge piece of melting butter on it. Along with that was a one-of-a-kind, never-before-eaten chutney which perfectly complemented both the items (and the only ones) on their menu. Any one who visits Mysore must surely experience this out-of-the-world pleasure.

After having a filling breakfast, we left to visit the house of the Wodeyar family - the majestic Mysore Palace. A guided tour was enough to tell and show us the entire story of the Wodeyars. Although the palace was beautiful, the fee counters everywhere were a big turnoff. We decided to walk around the city, one of the most well planned in our country. A sojourn at the Jaganmohan Art gallery was all that was needed to realize that it was time to rest. A quick-lunch at a nearby mall, and we were walking back to our cozy bunk beds longing to rest before we could continue our expedition through the historic city.

The majestic Mysore Palace

Zostel was again there to amaze us. An open library, an activity center, a swing to just sit and talk , an open terrace and so many people from different countries! Instead of sleeping for sometime, all three of us being enthusiasts ended up reading our respective novels in the activity center there.

In the evening, we had planned to visit the sand sculpture museum. As we reached there, we were disappointed this time by the season. We were told that the museum remains closed during the monsoons for obvious reasons and we were left thinking what to do next. Our taxi driver suggested that we go to the famous Chamundeshwari temple atop the Chamundi hill. Legend has it that 'Mahishasur', the king of the city then, was killed by the goddess Chamundeshwari here. Excited by the fact that we would be on top of a hill during sunset, we took the next bus to Chamundi hill.

Sri Chamundeshwari Temple

Once on top, we could see the entire city as the sun set and with it people slowly returned back to their homes after a long day. The view was so beautiful, we did not feel like leaving at all.

When the sky was robbed of all its light and we could see the houses lit up like small diyas, we knew it was time to return back. Luckily we were just in time to catch the last bus back to the city. And then it was dinner time! (my favourite part of the day üòõ) We went to another dosa shop near the bus stop and tasted new types of dosas and idlis we never knew existed. Vegetable dosa, Benne Masala Dosa and Thette Idli was the menu for the night. Some 8-10 bowls of sambhar later, three round tummied hippos started their long way back home. We were sad that we were going to be leaving this beautiful place the next day. So we were determined that we would make our time count.

The next day, our determination weared off and we decided to spend our time in the Mysore Zoo. Although it was not planned, it was one hell of a zoo! I had never seen so many birds, animals and reptiles in the same place. We were just halfway through the zoo when we realised that if we did not leave we would miss our train to our next destination.

We collected our already packed bags and left for the railway station. But we were not yet done with Mysore. We were not going to leave the city before having tasted the famous sweet of this place - the Mysore Pak. Again our taxi driver came to the rescue. He took us to the famous sweet shop of Mysore where we tasted the amazing, melting-in-the-mouth Mysore Pak. Of course we took a full box and boarded our train to the silicon valley of India, Bangalore - the first metropolitan city in our journey!
