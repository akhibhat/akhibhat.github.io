Having not had anything to eat after the Rajdhani lunch, the persistent rumbling of my empty stomach had woken me up quite early. In the dimly lit confines of our compartment, while the others were still in deep slumber, and with the windows closed due to the monsoons, it felt akin to living in Heisenberg's uncertainty principle.

The uncertainty principle posits that the more precisely one measures the position of a particle, the less precisely one can determine its momentum, and vice versa. Similarly, as the train hurtled forward, while I had a sense of the train's velocity, I remained utterly clueless about our precise location; more importantly, about my next meal.

To disengage myself from this presumed quantum state, I walked up to the train door to greet Mother Nature. And boy was I rewarded!

I stood there for a while, completely absorbed in the ethereal scenery that lay before me. As the mist gradually dissipated, it unveiled a breathtaking mountainous landscape that dispelled any remnants of sleep from my eyes. A tap on the shoulder jolted me out of the spell I had falled under. Apologizing to the TC, I quickly made my way back to our compartment and settled in by the window seat, slipping right back into my reverie until the train chugged into Naharlagun around 6:30am.

Before we embarked on our journey into the enchanting state of Arunachal Pradesh, we were required to obtain a travel document called the Inner Line Permit (ILP). Aimed at preserving the indigenous culture, safeguarding security interests and promoting sustainable tourism, the ILP helps monitor the flow of visitors to specific regions deemed sensitive or restricted. Having already prepared for this, we submitted our documents (ID proofs, address proofs and purpose of visit), paid the fee and set off for Ziro.

Talk about the jounrney

The journey, marred by traffic interruptions and heart-stopping moments, had left us completely drained. We somehow dragged ourselves to our hotel rooms and immediately collapsed into bed, waking up only for a quick room-service dinner. (A sentence that we woke up refreshed next morning)
